A Voter's Guide to the 2025 Texas Amendments

Based on the Taylor County Sample Ballot

What's On the Ballot?

This year, there are 17 proposed amendments to the Texas Constitution, falling into several key categories. The majority of proposals (10 of 17) are related to property tax exemptions and other tax prohibitions. The chart below shows the breakdown of all proposals by their main topic.

Visualizing Prop 13: Homestead Exemption

Proposition 13 is a straightforward proposal to increase the general school district homestead exemption. This exemption lowers the taxable value of your primary residence. This bar chart shows the proposed increase compared to the current exemption amount.

Tax Prohibitions (New Bans)

These proposals aim to amend the constitution to explicitly ban certain types of taxes that Texas does not currently have, securing their prohibition for the future.

Prop 2

Logic: To prevent future legislatures from imposing a state tax on an individual's "capital gains" (profit from selling an asset like stock), which Texas currently does not have.

"The constitutional amendment prohibiting the imposition of a tax on the realized or unrealized capital gains of an individual, family, estate, or trust."
Prop 6

Logic: To attract financial firms by constitutionally banning a tax on stock market transactions (a "securities transaction tax"), which is not currently levied in Texas.

"The constitutional amendment prohibiting the legislature from enacting a law imposing an occupation tax on certain entities that enter into transactions conveying securities..."
Prop 8

Logic: To constitutionally ban any future "death tax" (also known as an estate or inheritance tax), which Texas does not currently impose.

"The constitutional amendment to prohibit the legislature from imposing death taxes applicable to a decedent's property or the transfer of an estate, inheritance, legacy, succession, or gift."

Property Tax Exemptions (Cuts)

This is the largest group of proposals. They all authorize new or increased property tax exemptions, which would lower property tax bills for specific groups or types of property.

Prop 5

Logic: To lower costs for farmers and ranchers by exempting retail animal feed from property taxes, closing a gap where feed is taxed as business inventory.

"The constitutional amendment authorizing the legislature to exempt from ad valorem taxation tangible personal property consisting of animal feed held by the owner... for sale at retail"
Prop 7

Logic: To expand property tax exemptions for surviving spouses of veterans who died from a service-connected condition, even if the veteran was not rated 100% disabled.

"...authorizing the legislature to provide for an exemption from ad valorem taxation of all or part of the market value of the residence homestead of the surviving spouse of a veteran who died as a result of a condition or disease... presumed under federal law to have been service-connected."
Prop 9

Logic: To provide a tax cut for small businesses by exempting a portion of the value of "tangible personal property" (like equipment, furniture, and inventory) from property taxes.

"...authorize the legislature to exempt from ad valorem taxation a portion of the market value of tangible personal property a person owns that is held or used for the production of income."
Prop 10

Logic: To provide temporary, compassionate tax relief to homeowners whose homes are completely destroyed by a fire, exempting the value of the structure while it is rebuilt.

"...authorize the legislature to provide for a temporary exemption from ad valorem taxation of the appraised value of an improvement to a residence homestead that is completely destroyed by a fire."
Prop 11

Logic: To provide additional property tax relief for seniors and disabled homeowners by allowing the legislature to increase their special school district homestead exemption amount.

"...authorizing the legislature to increase the amount of the exemption from ad valorem taxation by a school district... of the residence homestead of a person who is elderly or disabled."
Prop 13

Logic: To provide a broad-based property tax cut for all homeowners by increasing the general school district homestead exemption from $100,000 to $140,000.

"The constitutional amendment to increase the amount of the exemption of residence homesteads from ad valorem taxation by a school district from $100,000 to $140,000."
Prop 17

Logic: To provide a property tax exemption for landowners on the Texas-Mexico border for the value of any border security infrastructure (like walls or fences) built on their property.

"...authorize the legislature to provide for an exemption from ad valorem taxation... of real property located in a county that borders the United Mexican States that arises from the installation or construction on the property of border security infrastructure..."

State Funds, Education & Health

These proposals create or dedicate large state funds for technical education, water infrastructure, and dementia research.

Prop 1

Logic: To create a reliable, long-term funding source for the Texas State Technical College system to update facilities and equipment for high-demand workforce training.

"The constitutional amendment providing for the creation of the permanent technical institution infrastructure fund... to support the capital needs of educational programs offered by the Texas State Technical College System."
Prop 4

Logic: To address future water shortages by dedicating a portion of state sales tax ($1 billion/year) to a new fund for water infrastructure repairs and new water source projects.

"The constitutional amendment to dedicate a portion of the revenue derived from state sales and use taxes to the Texas water fund and to provide for the allocation and use of that revenue."
Prop 14

Logic: To create a $3 billion state-funded institute, similar to the state's cancer research fund (CPRIT), to support research and treatment for dementia, Alzheimer's, and Parkinson's.

"The constitutional amendment providing for the establishment of the Dementia Prevention and Research Institute of Texas... and transferring to that fund $3 billion from state general revenue."

Government & Legal Process

These proposals address legal procedures and fundamental rights, including bail, judicial conduct, parental rights, and voter qualifications.

Prop 3

Logic: To give judges more power to deny bail to individuals accused of certain violent felonies if they are deemed a future danger to the community, a standard that is currently very high.

"The constitutional amendment requiring the denial of bail under certain circumstances to persons accused of certain offenses punishable as a felony."
Prop 12

Logic: To update the rules and membership of the state commission that investigates judicial misconduct, giving it more effective tools to sanction or remove judges.

"The constitutional amendment regarding the membership of the State Commission on Judicial Conduct, the membership of the tribunal to review the commission's recommendations, and the authority of the commission... to more effectively sanction judges..."
Prop 15

Logic: To elevate "parental rights" (to make decisions on their child's upbringing, education, etc.) from case law (set by judges) into the state constitution itself for stronger legal protection.

"The constitutional amendment affirming that parents are the primary decision makers for their children."
Prop 16

Logic: To explicitly add language to the state constitution that "a voter must be a United States citizen," which is already state and federal law, to serve as a clarification.

"The constitutional amendment clarifying that a voter must be a United States citizen."
